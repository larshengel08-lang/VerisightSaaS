# READ_ROUTE_COMMITMENT_CANON

Last updated: 2026-04-30
Status: active
Source of truth: this document is the leading boundary canon for dashboard, report, management summary and Action Center.

## Title

Read, route-opening and commitment canon

## Summary

Verisight uses one suite-wide boundary:

- `dashboard`, `report` and `management summary` are read layers
- `route-opening` may be explicit outside Action Center
- Action Center is the only commitment layer

This means:

- `read` signals and interprets
- `route-opening` makes follow-up legitimate without pre-filling commitment
- `commitment` starts only when bounded follow-through becomes explicit in Action Center

This canon overrides older truth that made dashboard or report carry default eigenaarschap, eerste-actielogica or reviewdiscipline.

## Canonical boundary

### Read

Read exists to show:

- what stands out
- what deviates
- what needs attention
- what first looks worth testing
- what direction may be logical next
- what interpretive caution still applies

Read does not, by default:

- bepalen wie `eigenaar` wordt
- de `eerste stap` vastleggen
- een `reviewmoment` prikken
- bepalen wie moet aanschuiven
- impliceren dat commitment al bestaat

### Route-opening

Route-opening may exist in dashboard, report and management summary when the product already knows follow-up is legitimate.

Route-opening may therefore say:

- this signal is route-open worthy
- this department or segment is locally relevant
- this follow-up direction looks like the best first route

Route-opening still does not equal commitment. It opens the door to follow-up without pre-filling the follow-through contract.

### Commitment

Commitment starts only when a bounded follow-through object is opened in Action Center.

Action Center is the exclusive home of:

- `eigenaar`
- `eerste stap`
- `reviewmoment`
- `opvolgroute`
- `vervolgbesluit`
- `afsluiting`
- `besluithistorie`

## Product truth by layer

### Dashboard

Dashboard is canonically:

- a light interpretation and prioritization layer
- a place for management read
- a place to show route-open worthy signals

Dashboard is not canonically:

- the place where commitment is assigned
- a mini Action Center
- a default action-planning surface

### Report and management summary

Report and management summary are canonically:

- formal interpretation and handoff layers
- stronger than raw analytics
- explicit about local relevance, verification and management caution

Report and management summary are not canonically:

- execution plans
- assignment sheets
- review trackers
- implicit commitment pages

### Action Center

Action Center is canonically:

- the only explicit commitment layer
- the place where bounded follow-through stays visible until closed or consciously stopped
- the place where route-opening becomes owned follow-through

## Segmentread and local follow-up

Segmentread has three states:

- `hidden`: not shown when n, privacy or relevance do not support it
- `visible read`: shown as context, without local commitment
- `route-open worthy`: shown strongly enough to justify follow-up

Rules:

- dashboard may show a department or segment as noteworthy
- dashboard or report may say a local route looks justified
- HR or management may decide that local follow-up is legitimate
- alleen Action Center legt dan `eigenaar`, `eerste stap`, `reviewmoment` en `vervolgbesluit` expliciet vast
- managers do not receive half-complete commitment logic in dashboard or report

## Language boundary

Preferred read-layer terms:

- `aandachtspunt`
- `eerste toetsvraag`
- `mogelijke vervolgrichting`
- `lokale relevantie`
- `verificatie`
- `bestuurlijke duiding`
- `interpretatiegrens`

Action Center-only terms:

- `eigenaar`
- `eerste stap`
- `reviewmoment`
- `opvolgroute`
- `vervolgbesluit`
- `afsluiting`
- `besluithistorie`

Read-layer terms to remove or soften:

- `eerste eigenaar`
- `eerste actie`
- `actieplan`
- `wie moet aan tafel`
- `eerste managementsessie`
- `30-90 dagenroute`
- `Eerste route & actie`
