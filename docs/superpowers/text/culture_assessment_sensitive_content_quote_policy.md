# Loep Culture Assessment Sensitive Content & Quote Policy

**Date:** 2026-05-20  
**Status:** text-governance safety artifact  
**Primary scope:** `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`  
**Canonical route id:** `culture_assessment`

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines what text must be suppressed and how quote use remains bounded.

---

## 2. Sensitive Content Classes

The route must explicitly suppress:

- names
- personal health details
- uniquely identifying incidents
- legal allegations
- disciplinary cases
- content that can identify a small local subgroup

If sensitive content appears, the route should prefer:

- suppression
- generalized wording
- cluster-level summary

Never:

- raw quote exposure in executive outputs
- raw quote exposure in manager-limited outputs
- pseudo-anonymized examples that remain reconstructable

---

## 3. Text Safety States

The text layer must use one canonical safety state:

- `not_collected`
- `collected_not_processed`
- `processed_safe_none_visible`
- `processed_safe_summary_visible`
- `suppressed_below_threshold`
- `suppressed_sensitive_content`
- `suppressed_unapproved`

---

## 4. Quote Policy

Default route policy:

- no raw quotes in executive outputs
- no raw quotes in manager cascade outputs
- no raw quotes in normal governed HR outputs unless future maturity explicitly approves it

Allowed alternatives:

- cluster labels
- synthesized summary lines
- "what participants often described" style wording

---

## 5. Sales and Demo Rule

Demo text must be:

- synthetic
- fictive
- or clearly marked illustrative

Real client text may not be paraphrased into demo assets without:

- explicit approval
- an anonymization policy
- confirmation that the result remains non-identifying

---

## 6. Approval

Approved when:

- sensitive-content classes are explicit
- text safety states are explicit
- quote policy is explicit
- demo-text rule is explicit
