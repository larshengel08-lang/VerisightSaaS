# Loep Culture Assessment Enterprise Trust & Procurement Readiness

**Date:** 2026-05-20  
**Status:** first-wave design spec  
**Primary scope:** `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`  
**Canonical route id:** `culture_assessment`

---

## 1. Purpose

This spec defines the **Enterprise Trust & Procurement Readiness** layer for `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`.

Its purpose is to make the route credible in serious buying, privacy, governance, and procurement contexts without pretending that Loep is already a full enterprise platform category incumbent.

This spec must answer:

- what trust questions a buyer may reasonably ask before purchase
- which answers Loep must be able to provide before making enterprise-facing claims
- how privacy, governance, exports, and role boundaries are explained
- what must exist for works-council and procurement conversations
- which claims are allowed, bounded, or forbidden

This spec does not create:

- unsupported compliance theater
- fake certification posture
- broad legal system redesign

---

## 2. Strategic Role

Inside the `Loep Culture Assessment` operating system, this stream exists because a premium boardroom product must survive not only sales interest but also scrutiny.

Without this layer, the product risks becoming:

- commercially attractive but procurement-fragile
- governance-conscious in product logic but underexplained in buying context
- methodologically strong but weak in trust packaging

This stream therefore turns trust from:

- implied discipline

into:

- explicit buyer-facing readiness

---

## 3. Core Trust Position

The trust posture for `Loep Culture Assessment` is:

- governance-first
- privacy-explicit
- export-bounded
- role-bounded
- claim-safe
- board-usable without overclaiming enterprise breadth

The route must not present itself as:

- a fully open employee listening platform
- an unrestricted manager reporting system
- an individual monitoring system
- a benchmark-first employee analytics suite

---

## 4. Trust Readiness Scope

The trust and procurement layer must cover eight topics.

1. processor role and data handling posture
2. privacy and export boundaries
3. access control explanation
4. retention and deletion posture
5. release and approval ownership
6. incident and issue handling basics
7. works-council and employee representation explanation
8. buyer-facing trust FAQ

---

## 5. Required Trust Pack

The V1 trust pack must be able to support guided sales and first-pilot procurement.

It must include:

- privacy/process role explanation
- DPA-readiness summary
- retention and deletion explanation
- export boundary summary
- access and role summary
- release and approval summary
- incident/escalation contact model
- works-council / OR explanation note
- enterprise buyer FAQ

It may be split across multiple documents, but the trust pack must read as one coherent answer set.

---

## 6. Processor, Scope, and Data Position

The route must be able to explain:

- what organizational data is needed to run the baseline
- what respondent-level data exists operationally
- what is aggregated into outputs
- what is never exported in normal product behavior

Canonical trust stance:

- `Loep Culture Assessment` is a group-level culture and engagement baseline
- outputs are designed for aggregate use only
- individual respondent data is not a normal decision surface
- no individual predictions or manager ratings are produced

---

## 7. Privacy and Export Boundaries

The route must clearly explain:

- minimum-n thresholds
- release thresholds
- suppression of unsafe segment views
- no respondent-level export
- bounded governed export logic

The trust layer must explicitly state:

- valid response does not automatically mean safe release
- closed baseline does not automatically mean every view becomes visible
- export availability follows governance rules, not user curiosity

---

## 8. Access and Role Explanation

The trust pack must describe, in buyer-safe language:

- which roles exist
- what those roles can see
- what those roles cannot see
- who approves release and governed access

At minimum, it must explain:

- executive
- HR partner
- business unit lead
- manager-limited
- admin

This explanation must stay aligned with the `Admin & Access Control` note and with the actual route behavior.

---

## 9. Retention and Deletion Posture

V1 must define a bounded and explainable retention posture.

This posture must answer:

- what campaign data is retained
- how output artifacts are treated
- how deletion or closure requests are handled operationally
- what is retained for governance, audit, or comparability reasons

The spec does not impose a legal policy itself.
It requires that the product route cannot be sold without an explicit and supportable retention answer.

---

## 10. Release and Approval Ownership

The route must be able to explain:

- who may release results
- who may authorize governed deeper outputs
- who owns export approval
- who answers threshold or suppression disputes

This must be a real operating model, not a vague principle.

V1 minimum trust bar:

- release owner is explicit
- governed export owner is explicit
- delivery owner is explicit
- escalation path is explicit

---

## 11. Incident and Issue Handling Basics

V1 does not need full enterprise security theater, but it does need a basic and supportable answer set for:

- where to raise privacy or access concerns
- who handles output or release mistakes
- how unsafe export or visibility issues are escalated
- how the first pilot handles incident ownership

This layer must be honest:

- do not promise a large enterprise support machine if it does not exist
- do define responsible owners and response path

---

## 12. Works-Council / OR Readiness

The route must be able to explain, in plain language:

- what is measured
- why it is measured at aggregate level
- why thresholds and governed release exist
- why manager ranking and individual prediction are excluded
- how open comments are bounded or suppressed

The OR explanation pack must help answer:

- is this a broad annual culture and engagement baseline
- how are anonymity and small-group safety handled
- who sees what
- what may and may not be done with the outputs

This is not a legal memo.
It is an operational trust artifact for serious buyer context.

---

## 13. Enterprise Buyer FAQ Topics

The buyer FAQ must cover, at minimum:

1. what the product measures
2. how results are released
3. what minimum-n thresholds mean
4. whether managers can see team-level scores
5. whether respondent-level data can be exported
6. whether open comments are shown raw
7. whether the product predicts attrition or behavior
8. whether the route includes benchmark in v1
9. how Pulse relates to the baseline
10. who owns approval for deeper outputs

---

## 14. Claim-Safe Trust Rules

Allowed trust claims:

- governance-bounded result reading
- no respondent-level export in normal route operation
- minimum-n and release thresholds protect safe aggregate use
- outputs are built for board, HR, and governed follow-through

Forbidden or unsupported claims:

- external certification not actually held
- full enterprise platform equivalence
- unlimited admin or workflow capability
- predictive or causal certainty
- unrestricted manager analytics

## 17. Out of Scope

This spec does not:

- certify legal compliance by itself
- replace legal counsel
- create broad security architecture for the whole company
- promise unsupported certifications
- define detailed product entitlements line by line

Those belong to legal, operational, or adjacent governance artifacts.
