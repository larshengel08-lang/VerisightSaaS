# Loep Culture Assessment Boardroom Deck Redesign Blueprint

**Date:** 2026-05-20  
**Status:** boardroom-deck redesign blueprint  
**Primary scope:** `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`  
**Canonical route id:** `culture_assessment`

---

## 1. Purpose

This blueprint translates the `Loep Culture Assessment Premium Output Visual Redesign` spec into a **slide-by-slide redesign structure** for the `boardroom PDF deck`.

Its purpose is to define, for each slide:

- slide title
- presenter purpose
- audience question answered
- visual center
- supporting copy
- speaker note
- governance boundary
- transition to next slide

This blueprint is intentionally limited to the `boardroom PDF deck`.

It does not redesign:

- the application UI
- the HR appendix
- the manager cascade handout
- benchmark layers
- self-serve outputs

---

## 2. Deck-Level Design Rules

These rules apply to every slide in the deck.

### 2.1 Format

- 16:9 landscape
- optimized for facilitated board-read
- exportable as premium PDF deck
- readable on screen share and in downloaded PDF form

### 2.2 Difference from the report

The boardroom deck must remain part of the same family as the `board report PDF`, but it must behave differently.

The report is:

- compact
- self-readable
- more textual
- print/download-worthy

The deck is:

- guided
- more spacious
- larger-statement oriented
- lower text density per slide
- paced for a facilitated board-read

### 2.3 Grid and margins

- 12-column presentation grid
- wider visual spans than the report
- strong top and bottom breathing room
- no widget-grid placement logic

### 2.4 Typography

- display tier for opening slides, hero slides and major transitions
- heading tier for slide titles
- subheading tier for governance framing, confidence cues and prompts
- body tier for short presenter-supporting text only
- caption tier for annotations and bounded interpretation notes

### 2.5 Color and chart behavior

- same warm premium token family as the report
- same restrained chart palette roles
- same suppressed / hidden governance tone
- no red/amber/green status logic
- no benchmark position logic

### 2.6 Narrative behavior

Every slide must answer one boardroom question.

Every slide must have:

- one visual center
- one primary speaking line
- one clear transition to the next slide

No slide may depend on dense reading to work.

---

## 3. V1 Slide Sequence

The boardroom deck v1 sequence is fixed as:

1. Opening / purpose
2. What was measured
3. Response and governance
4. Executive read
5. Culture Index hero
6. Domain picture
7. Board attention point 1
8. Board attention point 2
9. Board attention point 3
10. Segment contrast, if released
11. What not to conclude
12. Decision questions
13. Follow-on route options

---

## 4. Slide Blueprint

## 4.1 Slide 1 - Opening / purpose

### Slide title

`Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`

### Presenter purpose

- open the board-read with tone, scope and seriousness
- frame the deck as a guided annual baseline discussion

### Audience question answered

- what is this session and why are we here?

### Visual center

- full-slide editorial opening composition
- strong title block
- quiet contextual subline

### Supporting copy

- annual baseline framing
- directie / boardroom context line
- one short purpose statement

### Speaker note

- introduce the deck as guided, bounded and method-led
- set the expectation that interpretation happens within explicit governance limits

### Governance boundary

- no metrics yet
- no implied verdict before the signal pages

### Transition to next slide

- move from session purpose to the measurement scope itself

---

## 4.2 Slide 2 - What was measured

### Slide title

`Wat is gemeten`

### Presenter purpose

- clarify the fixed method scope before discussing results
- distinguish this route from broader survey or listening products

### Audience question answered

- what exactly does this baseline measure?

### Visual center

- fixed-method overview block
- ten-domain structure shown as a calm system view

### Supporting copy

- ten fixed culture and engagement domains
- annual baseline-first route
- no custom question logic
- no benchmark-first interpretation

### Speaker note

- explain that this is one fixed premium method, not a build-your-own survey
- keep the explanation concise and confidence-building

### Governance boundary

- no new measurement logic
- no self-serve survey workspace implied

### Transition to next slide

- now that the measurement scope is clear, show the response foundation and visibility rules

---

## 4.3 Slide 3 - Response and governance

### Slide title

`Respons en governance`

### Presenter purpose

- establish trust in the response base
- explain what can and cannot be shown in this session

### Audience question answered

- what is the evidence basis, and what stays governed?

### Visual center

- response strip as primary center
- governance boundary module as secondary anchor

### Supporting copy

- invited
- completed
- safe response basis
- release state
- hidden/suppressed layer note

### Speaker note

- explain that governance is part of quality, not a limitation pasted on afterward
- name the minimum-n and release logic in plain language

### Governance boundary

- `Wat getoond mag worden`
- `Wat verborgen blijft`
- `Waarom dat zo is`
- `Wat je hier niet uit mag concluderen`

### Transition to next slide

- move from the response basis into the executive reading line of what the baseline most strongly suggests

---

## 4.4 Slide 4 - Executive read

### Slide title

`Executive read`

### Presenter purpose

- give the room the first integrated reading line
- frame the discussion before showing the hero signal

### Audience question answered

- what is the main executive interpretation of this baseline?

### Visual center

- one dominant statement block
- compact narrative support beneath or beside it

### Supporting copy

- concise executive read
- one core management question
- one sentence on how to read the rest of the deck

### Speaker note

- read the core line aloud
- position the next slides as unpacking this read, not replacing it with data overload

### Governance boundary

- no causal proof
- no recommendation engine framing

### Transition to next slide

- move from the reading line to the central navigation signal: the Culture Index

---

## 4.5 Slide 5 - Culture Index hero

### Slide title

`Loep Culture Index`

### Presenter purpose

- make the Culture Index the visual anchor of the deck
- explain it as a navigation signal, not as a verdict

### Audience question answered

- what is the broad signal of this baseline?

### Visual center

- one large Culture Index hero treatment
- editorial signal composition rather than KPI widget

### Supporting copy

- index value
- short interpretation text
- one boundary line on what the index is not

### Speaker note

- explain that the index helps orient the discussion
- explicitly say it should be read with domains, patterns and governance

### Governance boundary

- no gauge
- no pass/fail
- no benchmark position
- no overall culture verdict

### Transition to next slide

- move from the overall signal to the domain picture that gives it shape

---

## 4.6 Slide 6 - Domain picture

### Slide title

`Domeinbeeld`

### Presenter purpose

- show the organization-wide profile across the ten domains
- keep the room in reading-sequence mode rather than ranking mode

### Audience question answered

- how does the broad culture pattern look across the fixed domains?

### Visual center

- editorial domain profile visual
- subdued score labels and short interpretation cues

### Supporting copy

- one reading-sequence note
- short interpretation line for the most visible pattern

### Speaker note

- walk the room through the domain profile as a guided read
- avoid lowest/highest rhetoric

### Governance boundary

- order is reading sequence only
- no top/bottom framing
- no ranking language

### Transition to next slide

- move from the broad picture into the first board attention point

---

## 4.7 Slide 7 - Board attention point 1

### Slide title

`Board attention point 1`

### Presenter purpose

- give the first attention line full space
- make it board-discussable without clutter

### Audience question answered

- what is the first bounded issue or tension the board should discuss?

### Visual center

- one large attention card or statement block
- confidence chip and verify-next cue in supporting positions

### Supporting copy

- attention headline
- priority reason
- what to verify next

### Speaker note

- explain why this point matters now
- keep it descriptive and bounded, not diagnostic

### Governance boundary

- confidence describes stability, not certainty
- no causal leap

### Transition to next slide

- move to the second attention line as a separate board discussion topic

## 4.10 Slide 10 - Segment contrast, if released

### Slide title

`Segmentcontrasten`

### Presenter purpose

- show governed segment differences only where release allows
- preserve the product's containment logic even in a visual deck format

### Audience question answered

- are there any safely released differences worth noting across approved segments?

### Visual center

- safe contrast module or governance-state panel

### Supporting copy

- short interpretation of visible contrast if released
- short note on hidden or unreleased layers

### Speaker note

- if contrast is released, keep the discussion bounded
- if not released, say clearly that contrast visibility remains governed

### Governance boundary

- only released segments may appear
- hidden or suppressed layers must render as governance state
- no sorted league table
- no best/worst segment framing

### Transition to next slide

- move from what is shown into what the board should explicitly not conclude

---

## 4.11 Slide 11 - What not to conclude

### Slide title

`Wat je hier niet uit mag concluderen`

### Presenter purpose

- actively prevent overclaim, ranking drift and false certainty
- protect the boardroom conversation from misreading

### Audience question answered

- what conclusions would be unsafe or methodologically wrong here?

### Visual center

- boundary statement cluster
- restrained warning-style module without alarm aesthetics

### Supporting copy

- no causal proof
- no manager ranking
- no individual prediction
- no benchmark claim

### Speaker note

- present this as quality discipline, not defensiveness
- keep the tone confident and calm

### Governance boundary

- the slide itself is the governance boundary module in narrative form

### Transition to next slide

- now that the room knows what not to conclude, move to the questions it should actually decide on

---

## 4.12 Slide 12 - Decision questions

### Slide title

`Bestuurlijke besluitvragen`

### Presenter purpose

- turn the deck into a guided board conversation
- frame choices without becoming a task-management system

### Audience question answered

- what should this board actually decide or clarify after seeing the baseline?

### Visual center

- decision-question matrix or editorial question blocks

### Supporting copy

- 3-5 fixed board questions
- short bounded context line under each question

### Speaker note

- facilitate discussion rather than prescribe action
- keep questions tied to the baseline evidence actually shown

### Governance boundary

- no action tracker logic
- no automated intervention path

### Transition to next slide

- move from decision questions to the bounded set of possible follow-on routes

---

## 4.13 Slide 13 - Follow-on route options

### Slide title

`Vervolgroutes`

### Presenter purpose

- end the deck with bounded route choice
- make follow-on feel governed rather than automatic

### Audience question answered

- what are the disciplined next-route options after this baseline?

### Visual center

- route decision matrix
- four bounded route cards

### Supporting copy

- no immediate next route
- deeper governed work
- Pulse follow-on
- another Loep route

### Speaker note

- explain that no route opens automatically
- stress that Pulse is conditional and not a patch for unclear interpretation

### Governance boundary

- no automatic continuation
- no workflow platform framing
- no open-ended action theater

### Transition to next slide

- this is the closing slide; the session transitions from presentation into guided discussion or decision

---

## 5. Global Forbidden Elements

The full deck must not contain:

- dashboard screenshot aesthetics
- gauge or speedometer semantics
- benchmark-position visuals
- league-table segment ranking
- winner/loser styling
- pass/fail framing
- unreleased layer amplification
- optional output framed as standard output
- dense text slides that behave like report pages

---

## 6. Global Acceptance Criteria

The full boardroom deck redesign is successful only if:

- the deck clearly feels like the guided sibling of the report, not a separate style world
- every slide has one dominant speaking line
- the Culture Index hero is clearly non-gauge and non-pass/fail
- the domain slide reads as guided sequence rather than ranking
- the governance boundary remains visible and believable across the deck
- segment contrast, where absent, still reads as a deliberate governance state
- the PDF deck remains legible, stable and premium in export form
- the full sequence supports a facilitated board-read without drifting into analytics-workbench behavior
