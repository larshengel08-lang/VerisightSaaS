# Loep Culture Assessment Methodology Authority System

**Date:** 2026-05-20  
**Status:** first-wave design spec  
**Primary scope:** `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`  
**Canonical route id:** `culture_assessment`

---

## 1. Purpose

This spec defines the **Methodology Authority System** for `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`.

Its purpose is to make the product methodologically credible in serious board and HR buying contexts without pretending to be Gallup, Culture Amp, Qualtrics, or Perceptyx.

The system must answer:

- what the instrument is actually measuring
- why these domains exist
- how the `Loep Culture Index` should and should not be read
- what counts as a valid response set
- what the product does not claim to prove
- how the questionnaire and scoring model evolve over time without breaking comparability

This spec is not a psychometric whitepaper. It is the operational product specification that makes methodology:

- internally consistent
- commercially explainable
- governance-safe
- versionable
- ready for later evidence strengthening

---

## 2. Strategic Role

Inside the `Loep Culture Assessment` operating system, Methodology Authority is the first major credibility gap between a strong V1 product and a serious top-tier assessment.

Without this layer, Loep risks becoming:

- a well-designed survey product without a strong method identity
- a premium-looking delivery package that cannot defend its own measurement logic
- a boardroom product with elegant output but weak explanatory authority

This stream therefore exists to turn:

- `methodical sharpness`

from a positioning claim into an actual product asset.

---

## 3. Method Stance

The methodology stance for `Loep Culture Assessment` is:

- broad annual culture and engagement baseline
- board-level descriptive reading
- pattern-based interpretation
- no benchmark-first logic
- no manager ranking logic
- no causal diagnosis
- no individual prediction

The method is designed for:

- organization-wide culture and engagement understanding
- executive pattern recognition
- governed comparison between safe organizational layers
- disciplined follow-through after baseline

The method is not designed for:

- diagnosing individuals
- producing automated interventions
- proving cause-and-effect
- replacing leadership judgment
- acting as a generic survey builder template system

---

## 4. Management Question

The method anchors on one canonical management question:

> "Welke brede cultuur- en engagementpatronen vragen op organisatieniveau bestuurlijke aandacht, en waar zitten de belangrijkste verschillen tussen onderdelen van de organisatie?"

This question governs:

- domain selection
- instrument tone
- reporting structure
- board attention logic
- interpretation boundaries

Any future methodological expansion must remain compatible with this management question or be treated as a separate route.

- one fixed annual baseline questionnaire

### 5.2 Response validity layer

## 6. Domain Model

The domain model remains fixed for the current version of the route and uses these ten domains:

1. `engagement_involvement`
2. `trust_psychological_safety`
3. `leadership_direction`
4. `collaboration_alignment`
5. `workload_capacity`
6. `autonomy_role_clarity`
7. `growth_development`
8. `change_readiness`
9. `reward_conditions`
10. `organizational_connection_intent`

Each domain is treated as a **construct family**, not just a reporting bucket.

That means every domain must have:

- a construct definition
- a board-level reading rule
- a caution rule
- a relationship to the overall Culture Index

---

## 7. Construct Definitions

### 7.1 Engagement and Involvement

Definition:

- the degree to which employees feel psychologically engaged in their work, invested in contribution, and willing to participate actively in organizational life

Board-level reading:

- this is an involvement signal, not a loyalty verdict

Caution:

- lower visible involvement does not prove disengagement as an identity trait or predict resignation on its own

### 7.2 Trust and Psychological Safety

Definition:

- the degree to which employees experience openness, relational trust, and enough safety to express concerns, ideas, or uncertainty

Board-level reading:

- this is a climate signal about voice and trust conditions

Caution:

- this does not prove legal safety, interpersonal health, or misconduct on its own

### 7.3 Leadership and Direction

Definition:

- the degree to which leadership direction feels clear, understandable, and organizationally coherent

Board-level reading:

- this is a clarity and orientation signal, not a leadership performance score

Caution:

- lower readings do not prove leadership failure at individual level

### 7.4 Collaboration and Alignment

Definition:

- the degree to which employees experience practical coordination, shared direction, and effective cooperation across teams or units

Board-level reading:

- this is an operating coherence signal

Caution:

- lower readings do not identify one team or function as the cause

### 7.5 Workload and Capacity

Definition:

- the degree to which workload feels manageable and supported relative to the capacity available to do the work well

Board-level reading:

- this is a pressure and sustainability signal

Caution:

- it does not by itself prove burnout, unsafe labor conditions, or staffing cause

### 7.6 Autonomy and Role Clarity

Definition:

- the degree to which employees understand expectations and have enough control or discretion to carry out their work effectively

Board-level reading:

- this is a role-architecture signal

Caution:

- low clarity does not automatically imply weak management or poor employee capability

### 7.7 Growth and Development

Definition:

- the degree to which employees experience meaningful development, learning, and future-oriented growth conditions

Board-level reading:

- this is a capability-development signal

Caution:

- it does not prove career stagnation at individual level

### 7.8 Change Readiness

Definition:

- the degree to which employees feel able to adapt to change and understand how change is being carried through the organization

Board-level reading:

- this is a change-capacity signal

Caution:

- low readiness does not prove resistance, disloyalty, or failed transformation

### 7.9 Reward and Conditions

Definition:

- the degree to which core employment conditions and perceived fairness around reward feel workable and credible

Board-level reading:

- this is a conditions and fairness signal

Caution:

- it does not independently validate compensation market position or labor-law adequacy

### 7.10 Organizational Connection and Intent

Definition:

- the degree to which employees feel connection to the organization and an intention to remain meaningfully linked to it

Board-level reading:

- this is an attachment and continuity signal

Caution:

- it is not a direct retention forecast

## 8. Domain Rationale

These ten domains are chosen because together they form a broad but governable annual baseline for:

- work experience
- relationship climate
- leadership coherence
- operating coordination
- pressure and sustainability
- clarity and agency
- development
- change capacity
- employment conditions
- organizational connection

The method is intentionally broader than a narrow engagement-only model, but still bounded enough to remain:

- board-readable
- repeatable
- suitable for one fixed annual baseline

This is also why the method does not add:

- custom modules by default
- a survey builder
- sector-specific question branches in v1
- an unlimited employee experience item bank

---

## 9. Questionnaire Structure

The current method assumes:

- `40` closed items
- `10` domains
- `4` items per domain
- one `5-point agreement scale`
- optional open text

The instrument must remain fixed inside a single version family.

That means:

- changing an item is not a light copy edit
- changing a domain is not a dashboard-only change
- changing scale behavior is not a local implementation detail

Any such change is a **method version event**.

---

## 10. Scale System

### 10.1 Closed-item response scale

The closed-item scale is:

1. `Helemaal oneens`
2. `Eerder oneens`
3. `Neutraal / gemengd`
4. `Eerder eens`
5. `Helemaal eens`

The scale must remain:

- consistent across the instrument
- understandable without specialist explanation
- stable across deployments

### 10.2 Reverse scoring

Reverse scoring is allowed only where it improves balance and response discipline.

Rules:

- reverse-scored items must be explicitly tracked
- reverse scoring must be deterministic
- reverse scoring must be tested
- reverse-scored items may not be written in confusing or double-negative language

---

## 11. Valid Response Policy

The method must define one explicit valid-response policy.

Current baseline rule set:

- minimum closed items answered: `32`
- minimum answered items per domain: `3`
- minimum valid domains: `8`
- open text: optional

Interpretation rule:

- if the valid-response policy is not met, the product may not silently create a normal domain or index reading

This policy exists to prevent:

- thin partial completion from behaving like full evidence
- unstable domain averages
- false confidence in low-completion response sets

---

## 12. Culture Index Philosophy

The `Loep Culture Index` is a **navigation signal**, not a verdict.

Its function is to:

- summarize the overall visible organization picture at a high level
- orient executive reading
- create a stable top-level board entry point

It must not function as:

- a culture health score
- a simple good/bad label
- an individual performance measure
- a causal proof layer

Canonical index reading rule:

> Read the Loep Culture Index only together with domains, response basis, segment patterns, suppression boundaries, and governance limits.

---

## 13. Domain Score Philosophy

Domain scores are more important than the Culture Index for actual executive understanding.

The method therefore assumes:

- the index is top-level orientation
- domain scores are the primary analytical layer
- patterns across domains matter more than one isolated low or high reading

This protects the product from becoming:

- a scoreboard
- a ranking system
- a simplistic average-reporting product

---

## 14. Interpretation System

The method defines four interpretation layers.

### 14.1 Response basis

- who responded
- what coverage exists
- where the method is allowed to speak

### 14.2 Domain picture

- what broad patterns are visible
- where the strongest visible tensions or strengths appear

### 14.3 Board attention points

- which limited set of issues deserve first executive attention

### 14.4 Governed contrasts

- what safe differences between organizational parts deserve further controlled reading

This means the primary logic is:

- coverage
- domain picture
- attention points
- governed contrast

not:

- total score
- manager comparison
- raw comments
- automatic action prescription

---

## 15. Board Attention Logic

The board attention system must remain deterministic and bounded.

For the current maturity stage:

- primary implemented inputs:
  - domain scores
  - recurring theme pairs

Planned later governed inputs:

- segment spread
- response coverage
- contrast strength
- safe open-text clusters

Board attention outputs must always include:

- attention point
- reason for attention
- confidence label
- what to verify next

Board attention outputs must never include:

- causal diagnosis
- automatic intervention advice
- manager blame

This keeps the product disciplined until later maturity stages justify more advanced logic.

---

## 16. What the Method Does Not Prove

The methodology authority system must explicitly maintain a **non-proof layer**.

It does not prove:

- that culture is good or bad in a total sense
- that one manager is failing
- that one factor causes another
- that employees will leave
- that one segment is objectively healthier than another outside the method context
- that open comments are direct evidence by themselves

This non-proof layer is not optional copy.
It is part of the method.

---

## 17. Evidence Framing

The method must define what kind of evidence the route actually produces.

The evidence type is:

- descriptive organizational evidence
- pattern-based executive evidence
- governance-bounded comparison evidence

The evidence type is not:

- causal evidence
- clinical evidence
- legal evidence
- predictive evidence

This framing should appear consistently in:

- methodology notes
- sales language
- board outputs
- HR outputs
- facilitator guidance

---

## 18. Versioning System

The methodology authority system requires explicit versioning.

Each method version must track:

- questionnaire version
- item wording version
- domain definition version
- scoring logic version
- validity-rule version
- board-attention logic version

Versioning rule:

- no silent method drift

That means any meaningful methodological change must create:

- a version note
- a rationale note
- a comparability note

Comparability note types:

- `fully comparable`
- `partially comparable`
- `not directly comparable`

---

## 19. Method Governance Log

The route must maintain a method governance log.

At minimum, the log should record:

- what changed
- why it changed
- who approved it
- what it affects
- what comparability statement applies

This log does not need to be public in v1, but it must exist internally.

The methodology authority system must be commercially legible.

That means Loep must be able to explain, in plain language:

- why this is a broad annual baseline
- why the method includes these ten domains
- why the index is a navigation signal

- pseudo-academic
