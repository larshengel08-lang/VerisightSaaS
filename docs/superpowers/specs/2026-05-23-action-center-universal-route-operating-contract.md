# Action Center Universal Route Operating Contract

## Status
Proposed

## Purpose

Define the enduring operating requirements that any admitted Action Center route must satisfy inside Loep.

This contract governs route behavior after admission. It does not approve route-family expansion, launch, pricing, GTM, or broader module claims.

## Fixed Boundary

Action Center remains:

- embedded inside Loep
- route-bound
- HR-governed
- manager-light
- post-scan follow-through only
- limited today to approved route families only: `exit` and `retention`

## Core Distinction

Two decisions must stay separate:

- `route eligibility` decides whether a candidate route may enter the Action Center frame at all
- `route operating contract` defines how the route must behave once it has already been admitted

This document is the second decision only. It does not determine conceptual fit or expansion approval.

## Reusable Guardrails

These guardrails apply to every admitted route and every route package built from this contract.

### Scope Guardrail

No route may use this contract to justify:

- route-family expansion
- launch readiness claims
- pricing claims
- GTM packaging claims
- broader module sprawl

### Operating-Surface Guardrail

No route may restate Action Center as:

- a broader work system
- case-management software
- workflow automation
- project coordination software

### Privacy Guardrail

No route may restate Action Center as:

- a broad employee case file
- employee monitoring
- a personnel dossier
- a risk ledger

## Decision Rights

- eligibility approval: founder/product owner plus route owner through the separate route-fit and activation-gate process
- activation sign-off: route owner and HR governance owner after the route package satisfies this contract
- escalation intake ownership: HR governance owner for primary intake and triage
- retirement decision: route owner with HR governance owner concurrence

If any decision-right role is not formally assigned, the route may not be activated until the missing role is named explicitly in the route package.

## Contract Rule

Every admitted route must publish a route package that instantiates each contract field below. The route package is a fillable operational record, not a second source of truth.

## Universal Contract Fields

### 1. Route Family

Each route must declare its route family explicitly.

Required rule:

- the family must be one of the approved Action Center route families active at the time of use

Today that means:

- `exit`
- `retention`

### 2. Route Intent

Each route must state the bounded post-scan follow-through purpose of the route in one short paragraph.

Required rule:

- route intent must stay inside bounded follow-through and may not widen into open-ended operating ownership

### 3. Bounded Management Question

Each route must define one bounded management question that the route exists to govern.

Required rule:

- one route, one management question

The question must be concrete enough that HR can decide review, continuation, and closeout without introducing a second framing layer.

### 4. Route Owner

Each route must name a route owner.

The route owner is accountable for:

- route package completeness
- route-default quality
- wording discipline against the reusable guardrails
- keeping the route inside the approved operating boundary

### 5. Manager Participant Model

Each route must define the manager participation model in bounded terms.

Required rule:

- managers participate through route actions and route reviews only

The model must specify:

- who may add or update actions
- what managers are expected to provide during review
- what managers do not own

### 6. Default Cadence

Each route must define a default review cadence.

Required rule:

- cadence exists to support repeatable route review, not ambient activity tracking

The route package must state:

- expected review frequency
- default stale threshold
- default escalation timing, if relevant

### 7. Action Policy

Each route must define what counts as a valid route action.

Required rule:

- actions are bounded follow-through steps inside the route, not a separate planning layer

The action policy must specify:

- what an action may represent
- who may create it
- when an action is active, stalled, superseded, or complete
- what action sprawl is out of bounds

### 8. Evidence Policy

Each route must define the minimum evidence grammar used to support route actions and route reviews.

Required rule:

- evidence must stay route-bound, minimal, and operational

The evidence policy must specify:

- what evidence is allowed
- what evidence is required for review
- what evidence is prohibited because it would widen the record beyond bounded follow-through

### 9. Review Policy

Each route must define how route review works.

Required rule:

- review answers the route's bounded management question using route state, action state, and bounded evidence

The review policy must specify:

- review owner
- review inputs
- review outcomes
- when review escalates to governance intake

### 10. Closeout Policy

Each route must define how the route closes.

Required rule:

- closeout means the route no longer requires active follow-through under its bounded purpose

The closeout policy must specify:

- who may close the route
- what minimum review condition must be met
- what closeout note or reason is required

### 11. Continuation Policy

Each route must define how the route continues when closeout is not yet justified.

Required rule:

- continuation keeps the route in bounded follow-through and may not reopen the route into open-ended handling

The continuation policy must specify:

- continuation triggers
- default next-step expectation
- when continuation becomes a governance concern

### 12. Governance Queues

Each route must define which governance queues may receive route issues or review outcomes.

Required rule:

- governance routing must remain explicit and limited

At minimum, the route package must state:

- the primary HR governance queue
- any route-owner exception queue used for route-specific operating exceptions
- any privacy/legal review queue used for privacy-boundary exceptions
- what issue types belong in each queue

### 13. Reporting Semantics

Each route must define how its state appears in reporting and readback.

Required rule:

- reporting describes bounded operating state only, not causal impact, employee judgment, or broad organizational performance claims

The reporting semantics must specify:

- how route status is described
- how action and review progress are described
- what continuation, staleness, and closeout signals mean
- what claims reporting is not allowed to make

### 14. Privacy And Dossier Boundaries

Each route must define the route-specific privacy boundary consistent with the reusable guardrails.

Required rule:

- the route may store only the minimum route-bound history needed for follow-through governance

The route package must specify:

- what route-specific content does not belong in actions, reviews, or governance notes
- what kinds of exception handling require privacy/legal review

### 15. Route Activation Checklist

Each route must define an activation checklist that confirms the route package is operationally complete.

The checklist must confirm:

- route family is already approved
- route intent is route-bound and post-scan
- bounded management question is explicit
- route owner is assigned
- manager participant model is explicit
- cadence, action, evidence, review, closeout, and continuation defaults are complete
- governance queues are named
- reporting semantics are defined
- privacy boundary language is present
- activation sign-off roles are named

Important rule:

- this checklist activates an already-admitted route package inside an approved family; it does not approve new route-family expansion

### 16. Route Deactivation Or Retirement Rule

Each route must define when it should be deactivated or retired.

Required rule:

- a route must be deactivated or retired when it can no longer operate inside this contract without boundary drift

Retirement or deactivation must occur when any of the following become true:

- the route requires a second unbounded management question
- the route requires repeated exceptions to the evidence policy
- the route cannot support clear closeout or continuation semantics
- the route pressures Action Center toward a broader work system
- the route creates recurring privacy or dossier ambiguity
- the route no longer fits the approved route family definition

## Route Activation Sequence

Use the following order:

1. confirm route eligibility separately through the relevant route-fit and approval process
2. complete the route package against this contract
3. review the package for operational completeness and guardrail compliance
4. activate the route only after the required sign-offs are complete

## Communication Hygiene

Demo or buyer-facing communication may exist alongside an admitted route, but communication hygiene is separate from core operability approval.

Any communication artifact must:

- describe bounded follow-through only
- stay inside the reusable guardrails
- avoid implying that an artifact alone proves expansion readiness or intervention success

## Plain-Language Summary

Eligibility decides whether a route may come in. This contract decides how the route must behave once it is in. The route package fills in these fields, but the enduring requirements live here.
