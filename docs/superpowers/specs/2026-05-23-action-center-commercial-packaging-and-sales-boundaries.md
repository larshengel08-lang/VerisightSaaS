# Action Center Commercial Packaging And Sales Boundaries

## Status
Proposed

## Purpose

Define the commercial packaging levels for Action Center without widening today's approved sales posture.

This artifact keeps packaging and sales language aligned with current product truth:

- Action Center remains embedded inside Loep
- current approved route families remain `exit` and `retention`
- HR remains the governance owner
- Action Center remains route-bound, post-scan only, and limited to manager participation inside an HR-governed review rhythm
- Loep remains canonical for route truth
- Action Center is authoritative for follow-through status, review, continuation, and closeout

This document describes three packaging levels for discipline and comparison. It does not approve all three levels for sale.

## Packaging Status Table

| Level | Packaging status | Current sales status | Route scope | Commercial note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Embedded Action Center | approved current packaging | approved to discuss and demo inside Loep | `exit`, `retention` only | this is the only active packaging level |
| Cross-Route Action Center | internal future state | not approved for external positioning or sale | no expansion authorized beyond `exit`, `retention` | may be discussed only as an internal future state |
| Standalone Action Center | internal future state | not approved for external positioning or sale | no standalone route scope | no launch, pricing, or separate motion is authorized |

## Global Must-Not-Claim

The following claims are prohibited across every packaging level unless they appear only in a negative boundary or prohibited-claim context:

- Action Center is `project management`
- Action Center is `workflow automation`
- Action Center is a `generic task board`
- Action Center is `employee monitoring`
- Action Center `proves intervention impact`
- Action Center `predicts individual risk`
- Action Center `improves retention`
- Action Center `reduces exit`
- Action Center provides `causal proof`
- `standalone launch`
- `separate GTM`

## Global Authority Split

- Loep remains canonical for route truth, route identity, and route scope.
- Action Center is authoritative for follow-through status, review, continuation, and closeout inside the approved route scope.
- Manager participation is always subordinate to HR governance and occurs only within an HR-governed review rhythm.

## Level 1 - Embedded Action Center

### Buyer

HR buyer, HR director, or HR operations owner who already owns scan follow-through inside Loep.

### Sales Motion

Attach Action Center to the existing Loep story as the bounded follow-through layer for approved post-scan routes.

### Demo Focus

- route-bound action follow-through
- manager participation within an HR-governed review rhythm
- HR oversight, continuation, and closeout
- visibility into stalled follow-through without broadening the product story

### Main Objection

"Why is this not just another add-on task surface?"

### Allowed Sales Language

- "Action Center adds bounded follow-through inside Loep."
- "It helps HR govern whether agreed follow-through happened, was reviewed, and needs continuation."
- "It is designed for the existing `exit` and `retention` route families."
- "Loep remains canonical for route truth, while Action Center is authoritative for follow-through status, review, continuation, and closeout."
- "It makes review rhythm and follow-through leakage visible without widening into broader operations software."

## Level 2 - Cross-Route Action Center

### Buyer

Internal product and founder audience evaluating whether a broader route family story may ever become commercially valid later.

### Sales Motion

No active sales motion. This level is for internal future-state discussion only and is not approved for external positioning, design-partner selling, or buyer-prep promise-making.

### Demo Focus

- show that the bounded execution model can be explained consistently
- reinforce that route-fit, activation, and live-evidence gates would still be required
- keep all examples anchored in current `exit` and `retention` truth

### Main Objection

"If this can stretch across routes later, what stops it from becoming generic workflow?"

### Allowed Sales Language

- "This is an internal future state, not a current offer."
- "Any broader route story would require explicit route-fit review, activation review, and live operating evidence."
- "Current approved route families still remain `exit` and `retention` only."
- "Any broader route story would still keep Loep canonical for route truth and Action Center authoritative for follow-through status, review, continuation, and closeout."
- "The model would need to stay HR-governed, route-bound, and bounded away from broad workflow behavior."

### Must-Not-Claim

- route expansion is already approved
- cross-route packaging is already sellable
- it is approved for external positioning or sale

## Level 3 - Standalone Action Center

### Buyer

No active external buyer target today. This level exists only as a future maturity reference for internal strategy.

### Sales Motion

No active sales motion. No separate launch, pricing, or stand-alone commercial process is approved.

### Demo Focus

- do not demo as a separate product
- if referenced at all, frame only as an internal future state
- return the conversation to embedded Action Center inside Loep

### Main Objection

"If it is almost a separate product, why not buy it separately now?"

### Allowed Sales Language

- "Standalone is not an approved commercial posture today."
- "Action Center remains embedded in Loep."
- "There is no separate packaging, no separate pricing, and no separate sales motion."
- "Any future standalone consideration would require later approvals beyond current buyer-readiness work."

### Must-Not-Claim

- standalone pricing is available
- it is approved for external positioning or sale
- it can be demoed or sold as a separate product today

## ICP Qualification Notes

### Qualify

- recurring route cadence already exists or is clearly expected
- an HR governance owner is named and willing to run review rhythm, continuation, and closeout
- the operating environment includes enough management layers that follow-through can leak without explicit rhythm
- the buyer feels follow-through leakage today and wants bounded governance rather than broad transformation
- the buyer wants a route-bound operating layer, not a broad software category shift
- the buyer does not show a broad workflow appetite

### Disqualify

- buyer wants a `generic task board`
- buyer wants `employee monitoring`
- there is no meaningful review rhythm to govern
- the buyer expects `causal proof`
- the buyer needs broad cross-functional work orchestration more than route-bound follow-through

## Boundary Rule For Sales Teams

If a deal requires route expansion, standalone packaging, standalone pricing, or proof claims to stay attractive, the packaging should step down to Embedded Action Center or the opportunity should be disqualified for now.

## Plain-Language Summary

Only Embedded Action Center is commercially active today. Cross-route and standalone labels exist to prevent sloppy storytelling, not to authorize broader sales claims.
