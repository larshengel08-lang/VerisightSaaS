# Loep Culture Assessment Design

**Date:** 2026-05-17  
**Status:** Drafted from approved brainstorming session  
**Product working name:** Loep Culture Assessment  
**Primary index working name:** Loep Culture Index

---

## 1. Purpose

`Loep Culture Assessment` is a heavy annual enterprise baseline for boards and executive teams that want a broad, organization-wide view of culture and engagement.

The product is designed to compete with:

- classic employee engagement and MTO-style surveys
- large enterprise survey platforms

The product is explicitly **not** designed to behave like:

- a lightweight pulse
- a self-serve dashboard commodity
- a performance or manager ranking tool
- a benchmark-first product in its first baseline version

Its core commercial promise is:

- provide a broad and sharp culture and engagement picture
- make that picture readable at board level
- surface governance-safe detail cuts where allowed
- support descriptive board prioritization without normatively interpreting individual scores

---

## 2. Product Role Inside Loep

`Loep Culture Assessment` becomes the heavy enterprise route for broad culture and engagement questions.

It sits alongside, not on top of or instead of, the current suite:

- `ExitScan` remains the route for exit interpretation and departure patterns
- `RetentieScan` remains the route for early retention signals
- `Pulse` remains the bounded review and repeat-check route

This means:

- `Loep Culture Assessment` is the annual broad baseline
- `Pulse` can later serve as a follow-up rhythm or quarterly review option
- retention or exit-specific questions should still be routed into the sharper existing Loep products when those are the primary management question

## 4. Default Delivery Model

### Default engagement model

- annual heavy baseline
- standard outputs: report plus board-read
- default collection model: organization-wide census

### Optional extension

- quarterly review or repeat-check is possible
- this is a follow-on option, not the product default

### Survey weight

- default instrument is a heavy enterprise questionnaire
- a lighter `volwaardig` model may exist later as an alternative package
- the enterprise model remains the commercial default

### Modularity

- the questionnaire is fixed, not customer-modular
- no theme packs or customer-specific module building in the standard product design

## 5. Measurement Architecture

### 5.1 Core logic

The product uses a broad classical engagement model as the measurement backbone.

It should cover a wide field including:

- satisfaction
- engagement / involvement
- pride
- trust
- leadership
- collaboration
- workload
- organizational connection and intent

The product still frames the top-level outcome as **culture**, not only engagement.

### 5.2 Primary summary layer

The product includes one board-level top summary score:

- `Loep Culture Index`

Design rule:

- the index is a navigation layer, not a totalizing truth claim
- it should help boards orient quickly
- it must not be sold or narrated as a simplistic "good/bad" end judgment

### 5.3 Domain structure

Below the top index sits a fixed set of domain families.

Working domain structure:

- engagement and involvement
- satisfaction and pride
- trust and psychological safety
- leadership and direction
- collaboration and alignment
- workload and capacity
- change readiness and alignment
- organizational connection and intent

These domains should be:

- fixed across customers
- stable across yearly runs
- rich enough to prevent collapse into a single summary score

### 5.4 Detail hierarchy

The result structure should support:

- organization-wide read
- location-level cuts
- department-level cuts
- team-level cuts
- manager-level cuts only under explicit governance

### 5.5 Open text

Open text is:

- optional per client
- never required for a useful baseline
- always secondary to the structured model

## 6. Board Prioritization Without Score Interpretation

The product may produce board-level priorities, but those priorities must come from **pattern logic**, not from simplistic score interpretation.

This means the product can prioritize based on:

- concentration of visible tension
- spread across organizational units
- contrast between units
- recurring combinations of themes
- density of internal divergence

This means the product should avoid language such as:

- score X is bad
- this number proves that manager Y is failing
- this index automatically means the culture is healthy or unhealthy

Preferred priority language:

- where tension is most concentrated
- where contrasts are strongest
- where a first board-level attention point is most justified
- where patterns recur across the organization

The principle is:

- descriptive prioritization is allowed
- normative score judgment is not the main reading logic

## 7. Output Architecture

The product should end in a premium board-ready package, not in a generic survey export or dashboard dump.

### Standard output set

#### 7.1 Executive / board cover

- what was measured
- who was included
- what the safe reading boundaries are
- what detail levels are and are not active

#### 7.2 Loep Culture Index layer

- one top index
- short explanatory framing about what the index does and does not mean

#### 7.3 Domain read

- fixed domain-by-domain view
- total picture
- relative weight
- visible patterns and tension areas

#### 7.4 Board priority layer

- limited set of first board attention points
- driven by pattern logic
- not framed as automated interpretation of a single score

#### 7.5 Organization structure layer

- location cuts
- department cuts
- team cuts
- designed as governance-safe deepening, not ranking theater

#### 7.6 Manager layer

- only when governance allows it
- not part of the default first read

#### 7.7 Open text appendix

- only when open text is enabled
- clustered and anonymized
- used as supporting context rather than proof theater

#### 7.8 Board-read session

- guided board or executive session
- purpose: structure the results picture and first attention areas
- not a free-form score interpretation exercise

## 8. Governance and Privacy

### 8.1 General principle

The product must be governance-grade and privacy-safe enough to support deep cuts without collapsing into a monitoring or ranking tool.

### 8.2 Threshold logic

Detail display requires minimum thresholds.

This applies to:

- location
- department
- team
- manager

Manager-level detail requires the strictest thresholds.

### 8.3 Named manager read

Named manager read is allowed only when:

- explicit governance approval exists
- stricter minimum thresholds are met
- the customer understands this is not a performance ranking layer

Named manager read is never the casual default.

### 8.4 Open text governance

Open text should be:

- optional
- anonymized
- clustered
- used carefully to avoid identifiable or overly literal readouts

### 8.5 Product boundary

The product must not function as:

- individual employee monitoring
- named performance scoring
- a manager score league table

---

## 9. Benchmark Vision

### 9.1 Not in the first baseline

The first baseline must not depend on benchmark logic.

The product should win on:

- own-organization clarity
- board readability
- governance-safe depth

### 9.2 Future-ready architecture

The architecture should still allow a later Loep benchmark layer.

Two future benchmark families are desired:

- anonymous cross-client reference layer
- sector / peer benchmark layer

### 9.3 Product framing for later benchmark expansion

Later benchmark framing should be serious and governance-safe, for example:

- Loep Benchmark Network
- Loep Reference Layer

It should not be framed primarily as a "competition between clients."

Proceed with `Loep Culture Assessment` as a premium board-level annual culture and engagement baseline that:

- beats classic MTOs by being broader, sharper, and more executive-readable

- protects itself from score reductionism through pattern-led prioritization
- leaves room for later Pulse follow-up and Loep benchmark expansion without depending on them for initial product value
