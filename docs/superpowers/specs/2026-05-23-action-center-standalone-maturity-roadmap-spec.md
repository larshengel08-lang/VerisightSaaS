# Action Center Standalone Maturity Roadmap

## Status
Proposed

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **strategic product route for Action Center itself** from its current Loep-embedded form toward a serious standalone product direction.

It exists to make five things explicit:

- why Action Center does not automatically fit every module or scan today
- how Action Center becomes a fully proven embedded enterprise module first
- how it can evolve into a controlled cross-route platform without losing boundedness
- what must become true before it can be treated as a serious standalone product
- which gates prevent premature expansion, proof claims, or workflow drift

This is a **strategic maturity spec**, not an implementation plan.

It does not:

- authorize route expansion
- authorize a standalone launch
- claim adoption proof
- claim causal intervention impact
- reopen approved Batch A, Batch B, or Batch C truth unless a blocking inconsistency is found

## 2. Current Position

After Batches A, B, and C, Action Center stands as a **bounded execution, governance, measurement-readback, and buyer-readiness layer inside Loep**.

What now exists:

- route truth remains canonical
- action cards exist as bounded route-bound execution objects
- managers can create, update, and review bounded actions inside approved routes
- HR governs rhythm, oversight, continuation, reopen policy, and closeout
- action lifecycle, transition rules, and draft / invalid / HR-review behavior are explicit
- governance queues and bounded HR interventions exist
- measurement readback exists across route, action, review, governance-signal, and route-family layers
- buyer-readiness artifacts, route-fit logic, activation-gate mechanics, and live-evidence gating now exist

What still does not exist:

- broad live operating evidence across many route instances
- multi-route-family proof of fit
- a universal route contract that can support many route families safely
- a tenant-grade standalone governance and administration layer
- a standalone commercial and category posture that has been validated in practice

Current approved route families remain:

- `exit`
- `retention`

## 3. Fixed Principles

These principles stay fixed through all three phases.

Action Center remains:

- HR-governed
- route-bound
- post-scan follow-through only
- manager-light
- canonical inside Action Center
- bounded in evidence, review rhythm, and closeout semantics

Action Center must not become:

- generic workflow software
- broad project or task management
- case management
- employee monitoring
- a personnel dossier
- an employee risk ledger
- a proof engine for intervention effectiveness

Standalone maturity does **not** mean abandoning these principles.

It means carrying them into a product that can stand on its own without turning generic.

## 4. Strategic Direction

Action Center now has a **serious standalone target direction**.

This does not change the current operational truth established through Batches A, B, and C:

- Action Center is currently embedded inside Loep
- approved route families remain `exit` and `retention`
- no standalone launch, route expansion, or product widening is authorized by this spec

This spec extends the roadmap by defining what must become true **after later maturity gates** if standalone Action Center is to become a serious product outcome.

That target direction is not:

- a horizontal workflow platform
- a generic action-planning tool
- a collaboration suite
- an HR operating system

The target direction is:

- a **bounded follow-through product**
- built around route-driven management follow-through
- governed through explicit HR oversight, review rhythm, continuation, and closeout
- capable of supporting multiple route families only where route semantics, evidence grammar, and governance rules remain compatible

This means the strategic question is not:

> "Can Action Center be made broad enough to fit everything?"

It is:

> "Can Action Center become strong enough to stand alone while staying deliberately bounded?"

## 5. Three-Phase Maturity Model

### 5.1 Phase 1 - Proven Embedded Enterprise Module

**Goal**

Make Action Center fully enterprise-worthy inside Loep before trying to broaden its operating footprint.

**What this phase must prove**

- Action Center is used live on real `exit` and `retention` routes
- HR operators can govern it at operating depth without excessive manual work
- managers can participate through bounded route, action, and review steps without dashboard drift
- measurement readback is interpretable enough to steer follow-through without overclaiming
- buyer-facing artifacts hold up under real rollout conversations

**Core outcomes**

- proven embedded operator use
- proven embedded governance use
- proven embedded readback use
- buyer and rollout confidence for the current approved route families

**What Phase 1 still does not mean**

- no route expansion approval
- no standalone launch readiness
- no evidence of causal retention or exit impact

### 5.2 Phase 2 - Controlled Cross-Route Platform

**Goal**

Prove that Action Center can support more than `exit` and `retention` without becoming generic workflow software.

**What this phase must prove**

- route families can be evaluated through one shared route eligibility contract
- new families can be admitted only through explicit fit scoring and activation review
- route adapters can vary defaults and evidence expectations without forking core Action Center truth
- multiple route families can coexist without collapsing into workflow exceptions

**Core outcomes**

- reusable route eligibility contract
- route adapter logic for any approved future family
- live evidence across more than one route family category
- proof that cross-route use does not cause workflow drift or privacy drift

**What Phase 2 still does not mean**

- no automatic route-family expansion
- no standalone commercial readiness by default
- no permission to weaken HR governance, review rhythm, or closeout semantics

### 5.3 Phase 3 - Standalone Bounded Follow-Through Product

**Goal**

Make Action Center capable of standing on its own as a serious product while preserving its bounded follow-through identity.

**What this phase must prove**

- Action Center can define and govern its own route contract across approved route families
- Action Center can onboard, activate, and audit route families without leaning on Loep-internal assumptions
- Action Center can support tenant-grade governance, permissions, activation control, and rollout discipline
- Action Center can be sold and explained as a bounded follow-through category, not as generic workflow

**Core outcomes**

- universal route contract
- standalone governance and administration layer
- standalone activation and route-onboarding discipline
- standalone commercial framing and category definition
- operating model for support, rollout, and route-family control

**What Phase 3 still must not become**

- project management
- workflow automation platform
- collaboration suite
- employee tracking or dossier tooling

## 6. Phase Gates

### 6.1 Gate from Phase 1 to Phase 2

Phase 2 may not begin unless all of the following are true:

- live Action Center usage exists across real `exit` and `retention` route instances
- manager action completion and action review completion data exist in real usage
- stale, sprawl, blocked, and repeated-no-progress signals have been observed in live operation
- HR chasing proxy data exists and is interpretable
- HR operators can explain queue behavior and intervention logic without inventing missing semantics
- buyer-readiness materials survive real buyer/operator review without workflow drift
- no unresolved governance or privacy incident tied to current Action Center execution remains open

### 6.2 Gate from Phase 2 to Phase 3

Phase 3 may not begin unless all of the following are true:

- multiple route families have been evaluated through the same route eligibility contract
- at least one additional route-family category beyond the current baseline has live evidence or is explicitly rejected for principled reasons
- cross-route operation has not introduced incompatible Action Center semantics
- route adapters have not forced workflow-style exceptions into core truth
- HR governance remains bounded and operable across the broader route set
- privacy / dossier safety remains defensible across the broader route set
- standalone readiness gaps are explicit enough to design directly, not guess at

### 6.3 Gate to standalone launch consideration

Standalone launch consideration may not begin unless all of the following are true:

- the universal route contract is stable
- tenant-grade permissions, governance, audit, and activation control are defined
- standalone route onboarding discipline exists
- live evidence demonstrates operating readiness across a credible route set
- the product can be explained without relying on Loep-only context
- the standalone framing is still clearly bounded and non-generic

This gate still does **not** prove:

- causal retention improvement
- causal exit reduction
- intervention effectiveness

It proves only that Action Center is mature enough to be considered as a bounded standalone product.

## 7. Why Action Center Does Not Automatically Fit Every Module

Action Center does not automatically fit every module because many modules produce signals that are too broad, too ambiguous, too people-sensitive, or too weakly owned to support bounded follow-through.

Action Center works best where there is:

- one bounded management question
- a clear route owner
- a small set of concrete execution steps
- a meaningful review window
- a legitimate closeout or continuation moment
- bounded evidence that is not dossier-like

It fits poorly where there is:

- broad cultural diagnosis without a route-bounded follow-through question
- many parallel themes that naturally want project tracking
- weak ownership or diffuse responsibility
- no credible review rhythm
- no closeout semantics
- high pressure toward employee-level monitoring or narrative storage

This means some modules may be rejected permanently.

That is a product strength.

The objective is not universal applicability.

The objective is **disciplined applicability**.

## 8. Route Eligibility Contract

A route family may only carry Action Center if it can satisfy the following contract.

### 8.1 Must-pass dimensions

- bounded management question
- post-scan follow-through fit
- route ownership fit
- action focus fit
- review rhythm fit
- closeout / continuation fit
- evidence grammar fit
- HR governance fit
- privacy / dossier safety
- no workflow broadening

### 8.2 Interpretation rules

- if any must-pass dimension scores `0`, the route is rejected
- if workflow-broadening risk is high, the route is rejected or parked
- conceptual fit alone is never enough for activation
- all route-fit decisions must still pass through live-evidence and activation gates

### 8.3 Consequence

Action Center can scale only through route families that **earn compatibility**, not through generic enablement.

## 9. Standalone Readiness Requirements

Before a serious standalone product decision can be made, Action Center must have:

- a universal route contract
- route adapter architecture for family-specific defaults without core semantic forks
- tenant-grade permissions and governance controls
- tenant-grade audit, sign-off, and activation controls
- standalone rollout and onboarding materials
- standalone route-family activation discipline
- standalone support and operating model
- standalone commercial framing and category position
- live evidence across a credible route portfolio

### 9.1 Commercial requirement

The standalone product must be explainable as:

- governed follow-through
- route-driven action and review discipline
- bounded execution after a scan or structured route trigger

It must remain impossible to honestly describe it as:

- project board
- workflow engine
- task platform
- employee monitoring product
- dossier product

## 10. Non-Goals

This spec does not:

- authorize standalone launch
- authorize route expansion now
- claim that Action Center already fits every module
- claim adoption proof
- claim causal intervention impact
- weaken live-evidence gates
- broaden Action Center into generic workflow or task software
- turn Action Center into case management or employee monitoring

## 11. Exit Condition Of This Spec

This spec is complete when:

- the three-phase product route is explicit
- every phase has a clear goal and boundary
- every phase gate is explicit
- the route eligibility contract is explicit
- standalone readiness requirements are explicit
- the spec preserves boundedness while recognizing a serious standalone target direction

## 12. Plain-Language Summary

Action Center does not need to fit every module in order to become a serious product.

It needs to become:

1. a proven embedded enterprise module
2. a controlled cross-route platform
3. a bounded standalone follow-through product

The path to standalone maturity is therefore not broadening first.

It is **discipline first, evidence second, scale third**.
