# Loep Culture Assessment Action & Follow-through System

**Date:** 2026-05-20  
**Status:** second-wave design spec  
**Primary scope:** `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`  
**Canonical route id:** `culture_assessment`

---

## 1. Purpose

This spec defines the **Action & Follow-through System** for `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`.

Its purpose is to turn the annual baseline from:

- a premium read with board and HR outputs

into:

- a controlled follow-through system for executive, HR, and bounded cascade use
- a route from insight to governed action without becoming a task platform
- a repeatable 30/60/90-day follow-through frame

This spec must answer:

- what decisions should happen after the board-read
- what HR is expected to structure next
- what managers may and may not do
- how follow-through stays within governance boundaries
- when Pulse becomes an allowed follow-on rather than baseline creep

---

## 2. Strategic Role

Inside the operating system, this stream exists because a serious culture and engagement product cannot stop at "interesting results".

Without this layer, the route risks becoming:

- premium in delivery but weak in follow-through discipline
- clear at board level but vague after the meeting ends
- dependent on ad hoc customer improvisation

This stream therefore adds:

- board decision structure
- HR follow-up structure
- bounded cascade logic
- 30/60/90 review rhythm
- explicit route-selection after baseline

without becoming:

- a workflow engine
- a project management product
- a manager performance system

---

## 3. Core Principle

The follow-through system follows one rule:

> The route must help the organization decide what to do next without pretending the product can prescribe, automate, or prove the correct intervention.

This means:

- outputs may structure decisions, not dictate them
- follow-through stays governance-first
- Pulse remains follow-on only where justified
- no action artifact may convert the route into a blame system

---

## 4. System Stance

The route treats follow-through as:

- one guided post-baseline management sequence
- one bounded board-to-HR-to-cascade handoff
- one explicit review rhythm

The route does not treat follow-through as:

- a generic action-tracking platform
- a task board
- a manager scoring regime
- an automatic intervention engine

---

## 5. Canonical Follow-through Sequence

The bounded sequence contains five stages.

### 5.1 Board decision stage

Purpose:

- convert the board-read into a small number of disciplined decision questions

Outputs:

- board decision questions
- what needs executive sponsorship
- what should not be over-interpreted

### 5.2 HR structuring stage

Purpose:

- define the governed follow-up frame after the board-level read

Outputs:

- HR follow-up priorities
- bounded deeper review areas
- escalation and support boundaries

### 5.3 Cascade stage

Purpose:

- support safe local communication where allowed

Outputs:

- manager cascade boundaries
- safe communication prompts
- what may and may not be discussed locally

### 5.4 Review rhythm stage

Purpose:

- structure what happens in the next 30, 60, and 90 days

Outputs:

- 30-day review focus
- 60-day review focus
- 90-day review focus

### 5.5 Follow-on route stage

Purpose:

- decide whether the next route is no follow-on yet, deeper governed work, or Pulse

Outputs:

- follow-on decision rule
- Pulse eligibility rule
- non-Pulse alternatives where relevant

---

## 6. Board Decision Logic

The board-read must end with explicit decision questions.

### 6.1 Canonical board decision template

The route must use a fixed board decision template with these questions:

1. Which one or two themes require board sponsorship rather than local interpretation?
2. Which visible patterns should remain hypotheses rather than conclusions?
3. Which follow-up questions belong to HR governed deepening rather than immediate action?
4. Which cascade messages are safe to communicate now, and which are not?
5. Is any follow-on route justified now, or is no immediate next route the better decision?

The route may support questions such as:

- what needs board sponsorship rather than local interpretation
- what needs HR structuring rather than immediate intervention
- what remains a hypothesis to explore rather than a conclusion to act on
- where follow-up should remain governed and limited

The route may not support:

- naming low-performing managers
- declaring the root cause
- assigning a mandatory intervention recipe

---

## 7. HR Follow-up Structure

HR follow-up must remain structured but bounded.

### 7.1 Allowed HR follow-up types

It may include:

- focus areas for governed discussion
- planning who needs to be involved
- determining which deeper outputs are safe and useful
- clarifying how local conversations should be framed
- clarifying whether a 30/60/90 review rhythm is sufficient without new measurement

### 7.2 Forbidden HR follow-up types

It may not include:

- turning HR into a free-form analytics program without governance
- back-solving causality from one baseline
- using the route as a disciplinary instrument
- mandating intervention recipes from one baseline read
- using unclear local signals as manager-performance evidence

---

## 8. Manager Cascade Boundaries

Managers may receive support for communication, not analytic sovereignty.

Managers may:

- use cascade-safe discussion framing
- acknowledge organization-level themes
- escalate questions that exceed local visibility

Managers may not:

- rank teams
- defend low local scores
- infer individual problems
- treat the route as performance evidence

---

## 9. Review Rhythm

The route must define a minimum review rhythm.

### 9.1 30-day review

Focus:

- board and HR confirm what is being followed up
- no drift into unsupported claims

Artifact:

- `30-day board/HR follow-up confirmation note`

### 9.2 60-day review

Focus:

- HR and sponsor review whether follow-up remains bounded and useful
- local cascade questions are checked for governance drift

Artifact:

- `60-day governance drift check and cascade questions log`

### 9.3 90-day review

Focus:

- determine whether a next measurement, deeper route, or no immediate next route is appropriate

Artifact:

- `90-day route decision note`

---

## 10. Pulse and Follow-on Rules

Pulse remains a follow-on only route.

### 10.1 Deterministic Pulse eligibility rule

Pulse may become eligible only if:

- baseline board-read completed
- selected theme is bounded
- owner is assigned
- review question is specific
- measurement cadence is justified
- no unresolved release or governance dispute remains
- Pulse is not being used to compensate for unclear baseline interpretation

Pulse may not become:

- the implied default after every baseline
- a workaround for unresolved interpretation
- a substitute for the annual baseline

Other follow-on options may include:

- no immediate next route
- deeper governed HR work
- another Loep route where the baseline reveals a clearer fit

---

## 11. Canonical Outputs

This stream must define:

- board decision question set
- HR follow-up note
- manager cascade follow-through note
- 30/60/90 review guide
- follow-on route decision guide

---

## 12. Enterprise and MKB Profiles

The same follow-through logic applies across profiles.

### 12.1 Enterprise profile

May include:

- richer HR structuring
- stronger governed cross-unit follow-up discipline
- later Pulse eligibility where justified

### 12.2 MKB profile

Primary value remains:

- executive and HR clarity first

Boundaries:

- simpler cascade path
- lighter follow-up structure
- no lite logic, only shallower operational depth

---

## 13. Ownership and Approval

At minimum, the follow-through system must define:

- route owner
- board-read facilitator
- HR follow-up owner
- governance owner

Approval must be explicit for:

- board decision framing
- HR follow-up framing
- manager cascade boundaries
- Pulse eligibility framing

---

## 14. Acceptance Criteria

This spec is complete only when:

1. the canonical follow-through sequence is explicit
2. board decision logic is explicit
3. HR follow-up structure is explicit
4. manager cascade boundaries are explicit
5. 30/60/90 review rhythm is explicit
6. Pulse and follow-on rules are explicit
7. enterprise and MKB profile differences are explicit without separate product logic
8. the next implementation plan can execute this stream without inventing new follow-through logic

## 15. Out of Scope

This spec does not:

- create a workflow platform
- create task assignment software
- create automated intervention recommendations
- create manager performance tooling
- redefine Pulse as baseline runtime

Those belong outside this route's intended wedge.
