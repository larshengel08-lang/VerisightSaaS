# Action Center Product And Founder Rollout Note

## Audience

Founder/product owner and any internal owner shaping positioning, launch language, or rollout approvals

## Purpose

Keep Batch C rollout decisions inside stable Action Center truth from Batches A and B, with clear claim discipline, route-scope discipline, and live-evidence discipline inside Loep.

## Acceptance Test

The founder/product owner can state what Action Center may be called, what it may not claim, and what still remains gated behind later live evidence.

## Sign-Off Owner

Founder/product owner

If no separate product owner is assigned, the founder explicitly assumes this sign-off responsibility.

## What Batch C Does

- packages stable Action Center truth for buyer readiness and rollout readiness
- makes product boundaries easier to explain without changing product semantics
- prepares controlled-scale evaluation without authorizing route expansion

## What Batch C Does Not Do

- does not reopen route, action, review, or governance truth from Batches A and B
- does not turn Action Center into a standalone product
- does not broaden Action Center into workflow, project, task, or case-management software
- does not create proof claims that exceed live operating evidence

## Claims Discipline

- Do not claim adoption proof from documentation or rollout materials alone.
- Do not claim intervention impact, retention impact, or employee-risk reduction.
- Do not present governance readback as employee judgment or proof of manager effectiveness.
- Do not describe Action Center as anything broader than embedded, route-bound, HR-governed post-scan follow-through.

## Route And Scale Guardrails

- Stay inside approved route families only: `exit` and `retention`.
- Treat Action Center as manager-light and HR-governed.
- Keep route expansion behind the later route-fit, activation-gate, and live-evidence process.
- Do not let commercial pressure weaken privacy, dossier, or monitoring boundaries.

## Sign-Off Expectations

Before approving rollout language or any expansion discussion, the founder/product owner must confirm:

- the language keeps Action Center embedded inside Loep and clearly route-bound
- the language does not reframe Action Center as workflow, project, task, or case-management software
- the language does not claim proof of adoption, intervention impact, retention impact, or manager effectiveness
- the language preserves HR governance, manager-light participation, and post-scan follow-through only
- the language stays consistent with the privacy, dossier, and monitoring boundaries
- any expansion discussion is framed as gated behind route-fit review, activation approval, and later live evidence
- no unresolved Batch A or Batch B semantic gap is being hidden by positioning language

## Launch Rule

If launch language only sounds compelling by making Action Center feel like workflow software, a proof engine, or a broader operating system, stop and rewrite the framing.

## Plain-Language Summary

Batch C makes Action Center easier to explain and safer to roll out inside Loep. It does not widen product truth, widen claims, or remove the need for later live evidence.
