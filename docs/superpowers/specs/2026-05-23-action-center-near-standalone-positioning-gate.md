# Action Center Near-Standalone Positioning Gate

## Status
Proposed

## Purpose

Define the strict positioning gate for any conversation that presents Action Center as operationally complete within Loep or risks drifting toward premature standalone language.

This artifact exists to keep buyer-readiness language aligned with current Action Center truth:

- embedded inside Loep
- limited to approved route families only: `exit` and `retention`
- HR-governed
- route-bound
- post-scan follow-through only
- bounded away from broad workflow framing
- Loep remains canonical for route truth
- Action Center is authoritative for follow-through status, review, continuation, and closeout

This artifact does not authorize a new product state. It defines the language boundary that must hold until later gates are passed.

## Gate Conditions

All of the following must be true before operationally-complete-within-Loep positioning can be used in controlled internal or buyer-prep contexts:

- Action Center remains explicitly described as embedded inside Loep, not as an independent product.
- Route scope remains limited to the current approved route families only: `exit` and `retention`.
- The explanation keeps route-bound follow-through, HR governance, review rhythm, continuation, and closeout as the center of the story.
- The explanation makes clear that Action Center is stronger than a passive reporting layer without widening into generic operations software.
- The explanation states plainly that Loep remains canonical for route truth, while Action Center is authoritative for follow-through status, review, continuation, and closeout.
- Buyer or operator language is limited to readiness, packaging clarity, and bounded operating depth rather than product independence.
- Any mention of a possible future standalone state is framed as an internal future state rather than approved external positioning or sale.
- The approved expression is "operationally complete within Loep." If someone uses "near-standalone" informally, the conversation must be corrected immediately back to the approved expression and to "embedded in Loep, not a standalone product."

## Gate Outcome

If every gate condition is met, the only approved outcome is:

- Action Center may be presented as operationally complete within Loep.
- The phrase means the product feels operationally complete enough to explain clearly, demo cleanly, and govern confidently within Loep.
- The phrase does not change packaging, route scope, launch state, pricing, or go-to-market posture.
- The approved expression remains "operationally complete within Loep." Internal preparation material should avoid "near-standalone" entirely.

## Explicit Non-Authorizations

This gate does not authorize:

- `standalone launch`
- route expansion beyond `exit` and `retention`
- standalone pricing
- `separate GTM`
- a new first-buy route
- category repositioning outside Loep
- `workflow automation`
- `project management`
- a broader employee record or operating system posture

## Required Positioning Language

Approved positioning should stay close to the following meaning:

> Action Center is a bounded follow-through layer inside Loep that helps HR govern whether agreed follow-through happened, was reviewed, and needs continuation or closeout.

Additional allowed language:

- "embedded Action Center inside Loep"
- "route-bound follow-through after a scan"
- "HR-governed follow-through rhythm"
- "manager participation within an HR-governed review rhythm"
- "operationally complete within Loep"

## Must-Not-Claim Boundary

The positioning gate fails if the explanation implies or states any of the following outside a clearly prohibited-claim context:

- `standalone launch`
- `separate GTM`
- route expansion is already approved
- standalone pricing is already available
- Action Center is `workflow automation`
- Action Center is `project management`
- Action Center is a `generic task board`
- Action Center is `employee monitoring`
- Action Center `proves intervention impact`
- Action Center `predicts individual risk`
- Action Center `improves retention`
- Action Center `reduces exit`
- Action Center provides `causal proof`

## Interpretation Notes

- "Near-standalone" is not an approved positioning label. The approved label is "operationally complete within Loep."
- If "near-standalone" appears in conversation, it should be corrected back to "operationally complete within Loep" and "embedded in Loep, not a standalone product."
- Loep remains canonical for route truth.
- Action Center is authoritative for follow-through status, review, continuation, and closeout.
- If a buyer conversation requires route breadth, category breadth, or proof claims to make the story work, Action Center is not a fit for that conversation yet.
- Any pressure to widen language should be handled by returning to the embedded Action Center story, not by stretching the claim boundary.

## Plain-Language Summary

Action Center may be described as operationally complete within Loep only if the conversation stays explicit that it remains embedded in Loep, limited to `exit` and `retention`, with Loep canonical for route truth and Action Center authoritative for follow-through status, review, continuation, and closeout.
