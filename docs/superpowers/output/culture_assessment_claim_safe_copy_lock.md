## 1. Purpose

This file locks the claim-safe output language for the `culture_assessment` output family.

---

## 2. Allowed Claims

Allowed:

- broad annual culture and engagement baseline
- fixed ten-domain model
- Loep Culture Index as navigation signal
- governance-bounded result reading
- safe contrast where thresholds allow
- guided board and HR read

---

## 3. Forbidden Claims

Forbidden:

- culture is good or bad
- manager X performs poorly
- this proves causality
- this predicts individual behavior
- this benchmark proves superiority in v1
- this is a free-form analytics platform

---

## 4. Per-Artifact "What Not To Conclude"

### 4.1 Board report

Do not conclude:

- that a single score is the truth about culture
- that a low domain proves one cause
- that local hidden layers are safe to infer

### 4.2 Boardroom deck

Do not conclude:

- that the deck is a manager ranking view
- that board attention points are prescriptions
- that safe contrast equals causal explanation

### 4.3 Executive one-pager

Do not conclude:

- that brevity means simplicity of interpretation
- that the one-pager replaces the method and governance notes

### 4.4 HR appendix

Do not conclude:

- that deeper governed access equals unlimited local access
- that safe segment contrast justifies local blame

### 4.5 Manager cascade handout

Do not conclude:

- that managers must defend low scores
- that hidden layers can be reconstructed informally
- that the handout is a performance document

---

## 5. Copy Source Rule

Board, HR, manager, and demo artifacts must all point back to the same method and governance language.

No output may invent:

- broader claims
- stronger certainty
- looser governance

than the method or trust sources allow.

---

## 6. Approval

Approved when:

- allowed claims are explicit
- forbidden claims are explicit
- every major artifact has a "what not to conclude" lock
