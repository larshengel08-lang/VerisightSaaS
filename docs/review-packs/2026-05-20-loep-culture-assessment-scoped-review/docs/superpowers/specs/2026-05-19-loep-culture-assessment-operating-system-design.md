**Date:** 2026-05-19

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **master operating system** for `Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld`.

It is not a single implementation plan and not a generic roadmap. It is the governing product document that should make four things true at the same time:

- the product has a clear strategic identity
- the product can be decomposed into capability streams
- each stream can produce its own spec backlog
- each approved spec can produce its own implementation or execution plan

This operating system exists to guide `Loep Culture Assessment` from:

- a strong baseline-only primary route

to:

- a serious enterprise-grade culture and engagement product

and later toward:

- a credible premium challenger to products such as Gallup, Perceptyx, and Culture Amp

without forcing Loep to become a self-serve platform clone.

---

## 2. Product Stance

`Loep Culture Assessment / Loep Cultuurbeeld` is defined as a **productized premium assessment** with software as its delivery and governance layer.

It is not defined as a software-first employee listening platform.

That means the core buyer value sits in:

- the baseline instrument
- the method and its discipline
- the executive and board-level read
- the governed drilldown layer
- the premium output pack
- the guided board-read and follow-on choices

Software exists to make that repeatable, governable, and scalable. It does not define the product identity.

This stance is deliberate, because the strongest wedge against Gallup, Perceptyx, and Culture Amp is not:

- the broadest platform
- the largest benchmark database
- the most admin tooling
- the most self-serve configurability

The strongest wedge is:

- **methodical sharpness**
- **board-level readability**
- **premium delivery**
- **governed drilldown**

## 7. Capability Streams

The operating system is built around ten capability streams.

### 7.1 Stream 1 - Governed Product Stack

Purpose:

- make `Loep Culture Assessment` a coherent, bounded, and governable product route

Includes:

- route identity
- baseline-only logic
- thresholds
- visibility rules
- result release rules
- exports
- route-to-route boundaries
- suite linkage to `ExitScan`, `RetentieScan`, and `Pulse`

### 7.2 Stream 2 - Methodology Authority

Purpose:

- give the product a stronger intellectual and methodological spine

Includes:

- domain model
- Culture Index philosophy
- scoring interpretation boundaries
- measurement rationale
- method note logic
- evidence framing
- recognizable language for buyers and delivery teams

Minimum top-tier capability expectation:

- construct definitions per domain
- item bank rationale
- psychometric support proportional to product maturity
- reliability view per domain
- valid-response policy
- confidence framing
- interpretation guide
- questionnaire and scoring versioning
- explicit "what this does not prove" system
- method governance log

### 7.3 Stream 3 - Premium Delivery & Output Excellence

Purpose:

- make the product feel like a serious boardroom product instead of a survey bundle

Includes:

- board report
- executive one-pager
- boardroom deck
- HR appendix
- manager cascade handout
- facilitator script
- output consistency
- delivery artifact readiness states

Minimum top-tier quality bar:

- board deck must work in an executive or board meeting without live dashboard dependence
- executive one-pager must stand alone
- HR appendix must deepen safely without breaking privacy boundaries
- manager cascade handout must prevent ranking and score-defense behavior
- facilitator script must support a reliable 60-90 minute board-read
- all outputs must follow one shared structure, one shared terminology system, and one shared interpretation boundary system

### 7.4 Stream 4 - Enterprise Drilldown & Analysis Environment

Purpose:

- make deep reading safe, useful, and enterprise-credible

Includes:

- segment analysis
- contrast reading
- suppression
- permissions
- HR drilldown
- safe open-text handling
- governed exports

Minimum enterprise expectation:

- organization hierarchy model
- multi-level segment model
- comparison eligibility rules
- permission inheritance
- view-level suppression logic
- small-n handling
- export entitlement rules
- release approval logic
- auditability
- "why hidden?" explanation rules

### 7.5 Stream 5 - Listening Program Operations

Purpose:

- make the product reliably deliverable as a full employee listening program across customers and over time

Includes:

- baseline operating playbooks
- stakeholder mapping
- launch readiness
- communication planning
- census-run preparation
- reminder cadence
- response monitoring
- closure protocol
- results release protocol
- QA and review routines
- turnaround discipline
- board-read preparation
- internal ownership model

### 7.6 Stream 6 - Action & Follow-through System

Purpose:

- turn the annual baseline into controlled management follow-through without becoming a generic task or workflow platform

Includes:

- board decision questions
- HR follow-up structure
- manager cascade boundaries
- action theme selection
- 30/60/90 day review logic
- route selection after baseline
- Pulse handoff where justified

Does not include:

- full task management
- manager performance workflows
- automated intervention prescription
- causal recommendations

### 7.7 Stream 7 - Text & Open Comment Intelligence

Purpose:

- make open comments useful without creating privacy, identifiability, or quote-theater risks

Includes:

- clustering
- sensitive-content suppression
- quote policy
- executive-safe summaries
- manager-safe summaries
- open-text thresholds
- theme confidence labels

Does not include:

- raw comment dumps
- individual-level inference
- sentiment claims without method support

### 7.8 Stream 8 - Follow-On Architecture

Purpose:

- define what happens after the annual baseline

Includes:

- baseline-to-Pulse transition logic
- governed drilldown continuation
- route selection after baseline
- expansion logic for enterprise and MKB

### 7.9 Stream 9 - Benchmark & Reference Layer

Purpose:

- define the future reference and scale layer only after the earlier streams are strong enough

Includes:

- anonymous cross-client reference
- sector and peer comparisons
- normalization
- benchmark governance

Benchmark-readiness rules that must exist before benchmark is activated:

- stable questionnaire versions
- stable domain definitions
- client metadata taxonomy
- sector taxonomy
- response population definitions
- normalization rules
- consent and commercial terms for reference data
- minimum dataset thresholds

### 7.10 Stream 10 - Enterprise Trust & Procurement Readiness

Purpose:

- make the product credible in enterprise buying, privacy, security, and works-council contexts

Includes:

- AVG and DPA readiness
- data retention rules
- access control explanation
- export boundaries
- processor and subprocessor answers
- audit and incident response basics
- works-council explanation pack
- buyer security FAQ

Does not include:

- procurement theater beyond actual operating capability
- unsupported security claims

7. Text & Open Comment Intelligence

## 10. Gap-Map Template

Each capability stream must be analyzed through one fixed gap-map structure.

### 10.1 Required gap-map sections

Each stream must answer:

1. `Current state`
2. `Strengths already present`
3. `Enterprise gaps`
4. `Top-tier gaps`
5. `Strategic choices`
6. `Current phase and next phase target`
7. `Required specs`
8. `Required implementation / execution plans`
9. `Dependencies`
10. `Out-of-scope boundaries`
11. `Verdict line`
12. `Maturity scorecard`

### 10.2 Meaning of each section

`Current state`
- what exists now in product, code, delivery, and canon

`Strengths already present`
- where Loep already looks strong or differentiated

`Enterprise gaps`
- what is still missing for serious enterprise use, even before benchmarking against category leaders

`Top-tier gaps`
- what is still missing to be credible next to Gallup, Perceptyx, or Culture Amp

`Strategic choices`
- what Loep does and does not want this stream to become

`Current phase and next phase target`
- current maturity phase and the next maturity target for the stream

`Required specs`
- which dedicated design docs must be created for this stream

`Required implementation / execution plans`
- which plans must exist after the specs are approved

`Dependencies`
- which upstream streams or decisions must exist first

`Out-of-scope boundaries`
- what should not be folded into this stream

`Verdict line`
- one short conclusion such as:
  - `not mature enough`
  - `enterprise basis present, top-tier gap large`
  - `strong differentiator, should be expanded`
  - `later scale mechanism, not leading now`

`Maturity scorecard`
- current maturity phase
- target maturity phase
- launch-critical status
- top-tier relevance
- confidence level
- next required artifact

## 11. Competitive Reading Frame

This operating system should compare `Loep Culture Assessment` against Gallup, Perceptyx, and Culture Amp through a consistent lens.

### 11.1 Gallup

Loep should read Gallup primarily as:

- the benchmark for methodological authority and category legitimacy

### 11.2 Perceptyx

Loep should read Perceptyx primarily as:

- the benchmark for enterprise listening infrastructure and governed analysis depth

### 11.3 Culture Amp

Loep should read Culture Amp primarily as:

- the benchmark for modern HR product usability and broader product ecosystem maturity

### 11.4 Qualtrics

Loep should read Qualtrics primarily as:

- the benchmark for enterprise EX infrastructure, analytics scale, survey operations, workflow breadth, and procurement credibility

### 11.5 Comparative rule

Loep does not need to copy all three.

It needs to define where it intends to win:

- boardroom readability
- premium delivery quality
- governance discipline
- methodical restraint and clarity

### 11.6 Category Capability Benchmark

Loep does not aim to copy Culture Amp, Qualtrics, Gallup, or Perceptyx one-to-one. However, to become credible in the same buying context, the product must be evaluated against a fixed category capability benchmark.

The benchmark must cover at least:

1. measurement authority
2. survey program operations
3. executive reporting and governed manager/cascade enablement
4. governed drilldown
5. open-text intelligence
6. action and follow-through
7. benchmark and reference readiness
8. enterprise trust and procurement
9. admin and access control
10. delivery repeatability

For each capability, Loep must define:

- category expectation
- Loep target
- what Loep deliberately will not copy
- current maturity
- required spec
- required plan
- launch blocker yes/no
